    I am currently a Visiting Assistant Professor in the School of Engineering Education at Purdue University. My PhD is in economics from University of Missouri-Columbia. My dissertation focused on empirical quantitative research methods and their applications to education research questions. I understand the needs of engineering students and genuinely care about engineering education because I was an engineer myself - I earned my bachelor's degree in industrial engineering from Tsinghua University.

    My research centers on understanding the pathways to academic success for students of all demographic and socioeconomic backgrounds with a focus on postsecondary success in STEM/Engineering fields. My research interests include: (1) the long-term influences of K-12 education; (2) K-12 teacher evaluations; and (3) faculty diversity.
